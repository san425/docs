## From Quizlet

1. Go to the Quizlet deck you want to import.
2. Click on the three dots icon below the flashcards.
<Frame>
<img width={300} className="rounded-xl" src="/images/quizlet-three-dots.png" />
</Frame>
3. Change `between the terms and definitions` to `custom` and set the value to `##`
4. Change `Between Rows` to `custom` and set the value to `####`.
<Note>
Do not leave trailing spaces after the values! It will not work.
</Note>

Here's what it should look like right now:

<Frame>
<img width={300} className="rounded-xl" src="https://help.omnisets.com/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F3145833720-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FE92lyML94tfclklF9yXw%252Fuploads%252FWH1NexwBRuDQr0EIkkk7%252Fimage.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3D455c5944-c6eb-4061-a706-dd33fa2203e4&width=768&dpr=2&quality=100&sign=07f1cbd3837c146f39b411d5d89cbcdfa0b3e588ff129a4d2ce3b68921e5dac8" />
</Frame>
5. Copy the text and head over to OmniSets.
6. Tap on the `+` in the top right.
7. Select `New StudySet`
8. Hit `Import flashcards`
9. You will now be redirected to the card editor where a modal will appear. Paste the text you copied from Quizlet into the modal.
10. Select the same separators you used in Quizlet. If you've been following closely, there should be `##` for `Between term and definition` and `####` for `Between Rows` as options to select.
11. Paste the text you copied from Quizlet into the modal and click Import.
